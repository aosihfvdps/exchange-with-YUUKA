# Primary sources
## [出口日語 YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@deguchi)
Japanese teacher in Taiwan.   
He teaches N5 and N4 in Mandarin Chinese, and N3, N2, and N1 in Japanese. 

## [Kaname Naito YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@kanamenaito)
Kaname Naito teaches Japanese in English.   
Kaname Naito explains ideas very clearly. He is very well regarded in Japanese learning community. 

## [Comprehensible Japanese YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@LearnJapanesewithNoriko/videos)
They speak extra slowly.   
Some of the [gaming videos](https://youtu.be/b48nA6dj8go?si=bYvlTs-okQ17ygf9) are very good.   
The speakers of some videos use sign languages at the same time, making the meaning quite clear.



# Grammar websites
## [TOFUGU Official Website](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/)
This is a website that has various of contents, from grammar, to vocabulary, to short stories, to introductions to life in Japanese.  
The provided link is to the grammar section.

## [日本語NET Official Website](https://nihongokyoshi-net.com/category/jlpt/n5-grammar/)
This website primarily focuses on JLPT. There are grammar explanations of grammar and quizes for every level of JLPT.


# Light Novels
## [小説家になろう](https://syosetu.com/)
In total Japanese. There are tons of Japanese light novels to read here. This website is the start place of many now-light-novel-writers. 

## [無職転生 (小説家になろう)](https://ncode.syosetu.com/n9669bk/)
From the same website above, this is the first edition (web edition) of one of the most well regarded light novel -- 無職転生.
