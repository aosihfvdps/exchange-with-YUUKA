## RYOSEI的回答
I think it had difference in nuance  
“変わりました” focuses on the event or action that caused the change. For example  
She quit her job. As a result, her life changed.  
“変わってますね” focuses on observation or the current state of being different.  

Use “変わりました” to describe the change itself or to explain something that happened.  
→ It’s similar to saying changed or has changed in English.  

Use “変わってますね” to describe the current state or to share an observation or impression.  
→ It’s similar to saying is different now or looks/feels changed  

But when we speak, we don’t really care about it, so you don’t have to care that much
